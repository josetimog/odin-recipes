Pork Sinigang Recipe

Description

Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup characterized by its sour and savory medley of flavors. It is a popular comfort food in the Philippines, usually served on its own or paired with steamed rice on rainy days to ward off the cold.

Like adobo, the term sinigang describes more a cooking method than a particular dish as it comes with many variations. It can be made with protein such as pork, fish, shrimp, beef, and chicken and souring agents such as tamarind, guava, green mango, calamansi, kamias, batuan, santol, and other native fruits.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork shoulder
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 medium-sized onion
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 taro root
  • 1 cup daikon radish
  • 3 tbsp Cooking oil
  • 2 finger chili peppers
  • 2 cups baby bok-choy
  • 1 packet sinigang mix
  • 2 liters of water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Pour cooking oil in a pan and let it heat up for a minute.
  2. Add pork shoulder and let it cook until it is browned.
  3. Add the water, tomato, onions, taro root, daikon radish, fish sauce and sinigang mix
  4. Cover and let the ingredients simmer for 20 to 30 minutes or until the pork is fully cooked.
  5. Uncover and add the finger peppers and bok-choy. Cover and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Serve with hot rice!
